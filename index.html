The Real Cost of Guessing

Blueprints look right on paper. They feel wrong when it's too late.

Change orders are the hidden tax of every construction project. A wall that needs to move. A kitchen island two feet too wide for how you actually cook. A bedroom that felt spacious at 14×16 on paper — and feels cramped in real life.

These aren't contractor mistakes. They're visualization gaps — the natural result of trying to imagine a three-dimensional space from a flat drawing. Nobody can do it perfectly. And when you're wrong after ground breaks, you pay for it.

10 – 25%
Industry data shows change orders average 10% of total contract value — and up to 25% on complex custom builds. On a $300,000 home, that's $30,000 in decisions you wish you'd made before construction started.
